Research

At CLO Virtual Fashion I work on deep learning for 3D garments. My latest project, DiffGI (ECCV 2026), is a differentiable geometry-image framework that turns a single image into simulation-ready thin-shell 3D. Along the way we also made garment simulation learnable, estimating physical fabric parameters from drape tests and tag information (Eurographics 2024, CAVW 2024).

Before fashion I worked on medical AI: deep-learning pipelines for CT analysis at KIST — automated cephalometry, rotator-cuff tear classification, mandible & maxilla segmentation — then led the AI team at Imagoworks, where our tooth-segmentation and crown-generation work for 3D dental scans shipped to digital dentistry and became granted US patents.

These days I care most about 3D generative models, 3D Gaussian splatting, and edge-device inference. The browser is my favorite research medium — WebGL, WebAssembly, neural nets running on-device — like the piece of cloth hanging at the top of this page.
